About FWW

Fostering Without Walls is a 501c3 that was created early in 2019. After starting Red Hawk Ranch, a group home for siblings removed by the Department of Child Safety, we realized many of the kids needs were not fulfilled by the State. The board members of FWW all believe in giving back to the community and making a difference in this world. We have a tender heart for kids and want to make their lives better while they are going through this traumatic time.

Board Members

Chairman: Kim Mccurtain

Kim was a probation officer for over 20 years. She was one of the leaders and trainers in risk reduction and continues to train new officers around the state for AOC (Arizona Office of the Courts). Kim is also the Chief Financial Officer for Red Hawk Ranch, a sibling home for 18 boys and girls removed from their homes by the State. She is a graduate of Arizona State University in Tempe, Arizona, where she earned a Bachelor’s Degree in Criminal Justice with a minor in political science. She is a graduate of Northern Arizona University, where she earned a Master’s Degree in Educational Leadership. She has volunteered on the Foster Care Review Board for 7 years. She volunteers to improve the health and wellness of Police Officers in the State of Arizona.




Vice President: Mrs. Jan Johnston

Jan left the cold winters of Nebraska in 1977 to pursue her undergraduate and master's degrees at ASU. Along the way she became an Adult Probation Officer for Maricopa County, where she honed her skills working a myriad of assignments, to include Investigations, Intensive Supervision, Court Liaison, Training, and finally working with the Seriously Mentally Ill population. She joined the Board of Directors of Triple R Behavioral Health in 2007. That same year she retired from Probation after 27 years. Triple R merged with New Arizona Family in 2011 creating the multi-service provider agency, Lifewell Behavioral Wellness. Jan served on that Board of Directors until August of 2017. As a long time cat lover, she became an active volunteer with the Paw Placement Animal Rescue in 2008. She has also served as a Foster Care Review Board member for over 33-years. Besides volunteering, Jan and her husband, Rob, are avid world travelers and scuba divers.




Treasurer: Linnea Tow

Linnea is a mother of 3 children, 2 step daughters and 8 grandchildren. She began her career working in the field of optometry in 1971 working as a scheduler, back up optician and pre-test technician. She retired from this profession in 2009. She has had a passion for cards since she was little. She started playing social bridge at a young age and transitioned to a competitive league in 1996. She currently is a board member for Bridge Unit 351. She has been a hospitality chairperson and is has been their treasurer since 2009. She looks forward to serving on the FWW board and helping fulfill the needs of the kids in foster care.




Secretary: Mrs. Carol J. Graham

Mrs. Graham graduated from Arizona State University, with a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice, in 1987. She was employed with the Maricopa County Courts and Adult Probation Department for over 24 years before retiring in 2011. While employed, she held numerous positions dealing with the investigation and supervision of adult clientele in need of community agency referrals addressing various issues including mental health, substance abuse, financial, life skills, and cognitive behavioral changes. Mrs. Graham served on various committees addressing these issues throughout her employment. In 2013, she obtained certification and licensing as a Certified Pharmacy Technician and worked in the field for two years. She has volunteered at local community agencies. Mrs. Graham brings to the board over 30 years of experience in social services with a desire to positively impact the children and the community of the Northern Arizona.
